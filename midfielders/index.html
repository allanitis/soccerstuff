Forwards

A forward is a player dedicated to scoring goals. They are the target of the offence to pass to. From the recreational level to the professional level, the forwards are almost always at the top of the goal-scoring ranks. There is some variant in the roles of forward players, however. The striker is the most commonly seen attacking player. They will linger near the center of the field, waiting for a dangerous pass to come through from the midfielders or wingers. The wingers are also fairly common. Their job is to stay near the sidelines in an effort to strech out the opposing defence. The center forward, or false nine, will look to draw in the defence to create more space for the wingers.

Position Name // Notation Role Notable Player(s)
Striker // S Score goals. Position themselves on the defensive line for long passes down the field, making runs toward goal. Harry Kane, Lewandoski, Luis Suarez
Left Wing & Right Wing // LW & RW Make runs down the wings and look to lob a cross into the box where a teammate can score a goal. If possible, cut towards the box and take a shot. Cristiano Ronaldo (LW), Neymar (LW), Lionel Messi (RW), Gareth Bale (RW)
Center Forward // CF Act as a fake striker, or false nine as the professionals call it, to draw the defense towards the center of the field to allow the wingers to have more space to make runs. Alexis Sanchez, Dennis Bergkamp, Johan Cruyff
